How a race works

Two teams of four race through four stages. Each stage is a different game.

  1. Solve your puzzle — everyone on your team gets their own board. Solving it turns you green.
  2. Rest — after going green you get a short breather (15s).
  3. Holding questions — if your rest runs out before the whole team is green, you get quick-fire mini-puzzles (20s each). Answer right to stay green; answer wrong (or too slow) and you lose green and get a fresh main puzzle.
  4. Advance together — the moment all four of you are green, the team jumps to the next stage.
  5. Win — first team through Stage 4 takes the race.

💡 A wrong answer on your main puzzle never costs you anything except time — you just get a fresh board. Speed and steadiness win relays.

Stage 1 ⚡ REWIRE

Puzzle · spatial reasoning

What you see

A 4×4 grid of wire tiles — straights, elbows, T-junctions and endpoints — scrambled into random rotations. One tile glows: the source. Two tiles are sinks.

How to play

  • Tap a tile to rotate it 90° clockwise. Tiles never move, only turn.
  • Powered wires light up live as you work, so you can see the current spread.
  • You win when power reaches every sink and no live wire dangles into a wall or a closed tile.

Main: 4×4 grid, 2 sinks · Holding: 2×2 grid, 1 sink

💡 Trace a path backwards from each sink to the source first — then rotate along the route.

Stage 2 💣 SWEEP

Logical · deduction

What you see

A 6×6 minesweeper board hiding 6 mines, with a safe opening region already revealed. Every number counts the mines in its eight neighbours.

How to play

  • Tap a cell you've deduced is safe to reveal it.
  • Right-click (or long-press on touch) a cell you've deduced is a mine to flag it.
  • Flag all 6 mines, then submit. Revealing a mine is an instant 💥 — you get a fresh board.

Main: 6×6, 6 mines · Holding: 3×3, 1 mine

💡 Every board is solvable with pure logic — no guessing is ever required. If you're guessing, there's a deduction you haven't spotted.

Stage 3 🧪 DECANT

Sorting · planning

What you see

Six tubes holding stacks of four colours (plus empties), each tube holding up to four segments.

How to play

  • Tap a source tube, then a destination tube, to pour.
  • A pour moves one block — the top of the source tube — and it can go into any tube with room, even onto a different colour.
  • You win when every tube is empty or completely one colour. You can't get stuck — but sloppy pours cost precious seconds, and Undo is there if you need it.

Main: 6 tubes, 4 colours, capacity 4 · Holding: 3 tubes, 2 colours (~2 pours)

💡 Keep an empty tube free as workspace as long as you can — burying a colour under two others is what costs you.

Stage 4 🔊 ECHO

Reflex / memory

What you see

A 3×3 grid of pads. They flash one at a time in a sequence of five — and the sequence plays only once.

How to play

  • Watch the whole sequence. Don't tap while it plays.
  • Then tap the pads back in exactly the same order.
  • One wrong tap fails the attempt — and you get a brand-new sequence, so there's no second viewing.

Main: 3×3 pads, sequence of 5 · Holding: 2×2 pads, sequence of 3

💡 Chunk it: remember "corner, centre, top…" as a shape traced across the grid, not as nine separate positions.

⚖️ Fair play, by design

Every player gets a different, randomly generated board — your teammate's answer is useless to you, and there's nothing to Google. The server re-checks every move you submit, so the only way through is to actually play.
